User Research

A local Atlanta Non-Profit Organization

Autism Improvised is a local Atlanta Non-Profit Organization that strives to assist people living with autism. They offer a soft landing space to practice and develop social communication ​skills through the magic of improv and theatre. We had the wonderful opportunity to work closely with the stakeholders of this organization and help them achieve their vision!

Problem

The user interface of Autism Improvised lacks clarity. It does not visually share the story of the organization’s mission which is preventing it from creating a strong, vocal digital platform.

goal

We believe in adjusting navigation, adding life to the User Interface and simply improving the user experience of the website will maximize our user activity.

Team & My role

We are a team of User Experience Designers and Researchers. Our teamwork guided us to work together through every phase of UX in order to gain clarity regarding the needs of our potential users and the desires of the stakeholders. TEAMWORK: Teamwork was extremely important for the three of us. Let’s just say, we came together to learn, design and grow!

MY ROLE: I was a team-player throughout the entirety of the project. My main focus during this project was the user interface, that is where my passion lies in our industry. This project allowed me to infuse my imagination and recreate a new world for Autism Improvised!

User Persona

“Meet Jalen Wilson, a grandmother who is searching online for a non-profit organization that can help her teenage grandson who is living with autism and is struggling to confidently build his social skills.”

Value Proposition

Autism Improvised provides theater and improv programs to assist those living with high-functioning autism, allowing them to bolster their social skills and confidence within social situations, fostering a more accepting and normalized view of the world the live will be thrusted into.

Initial User Interface Issues

The original website used more than six different fonts, which was a good route because it added a lot of confusion.

The original logo was outdated aesthetically. Out of curiosity, we redesigned the logo because we saw the potential of rebranding.

A New Vision

Maintain two fonts: Consistency was an important goal.

Continue using the main color, blue of Autism Improvised but add two new tones that will add life and a modern twist to the interface.

The of cards was crucial because they are visual cues that will indicate some of values and goals of the organization.

Style Guide

All of the images used throughout our design protrayed the social vibes that communicated the potential growth that future students may feel once they join the organization. We did not want to focus on the faces but more on the fragmented gestures, such as hand shakes, hugs, anything that gave off the essence of social unity and teamwork.

Iconography

The use of icons was extremely important to building the brand of the organization. We had to keep our potential users in mind, “high school students”.
